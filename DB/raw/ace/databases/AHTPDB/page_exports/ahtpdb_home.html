What is AHTPDB ?

Hypertension or high blood pressure is a major cause of mortality in modern world. In last decade, peptides from natural sources with antihypertensive activity attained major focus due to their effeftivity and non-toxic nature. AHTPDB is a portal for all possible antihypertensive peptides, which mainly included 'Angiotensin-I Converting Enzyme (ACE)' inhibiting peptides. Here, an attempt have been made to collect and present all known AHT peptides with all the relevant information e.g. Sequence, length, IC50, Bitterness etc. Attempt have been made to predict structure of each peptide having therapeutic potential.

Mandate of AHTPDB

Although a number of Antihypertensive drugs are avilable in the market e.g. Captopril, Lisinopril etc. but all such drugs are associated with adverse side-effects e.g skin rashes, kidney failure etc. Hence, those natural peptides/proteins which are known to have antihypertensive effects are of great importance. The main motive of AHTPDB is to collect, compile and systematically present all such Antihypertensive peptides.

Why use AHTPDB ?

In last decade, there is a surge in exploring all posible natural resouces for identification of potential AHT peptides. 'VPP' and 'IPP' are two such peptides which are identified from Casein(milk) and used in food as antihypertensive peptide, as food additive by pharaceutical industries. hence, it is imperative to present all such peptides which their antihypertensive activities and related literature in a single platform, giving rise of 'AHTPDB' database. We have collected all possible AHT peptides, which also included all known peptides in previous databases e.g. ACEpepBD or BIOPEP. We have collected the largest collection of all such natural peptides, also included allknown antihyperteive proteins too. Hence, it can be said that AHTPDB is a sigle portal available providing maximum information related with AHT peptides and proteins, mainly from natural resources.

Importance/Significance

In order to search a AHT peptide, its sequence, length-based searching, Inhibitory or toxicity based search, mol. wt etc, there are a number of ways in which AHTPDB may help a user to have quick information related these peptides. It will enable a user to find maximum useful information regarding AHT peptides in shortest possible time.

The application of antihypertensive peptides are numerous e.g.
(1) Therapeutic agents: Most of the Antihypertensive agents acts as ACE inhibitor and inhibitor converstion of Angiotensin-I into Angiotensin-II, which is potent vasoconstrictor. Thereby acting as antihypertensive agent or blood pressure lowering agent.
(2) Food Additives: These days a number of peptides are being synthesized and used as potent antihypertensive agents in various type of food. IPP and VPP are two such food additive peptides.
(3) Peptide Resources: This will be a major platform for systematic presentation of antihypertensive peptides with all relevant information related with these peptides e.g. source, IC50, pIC50, molecular mass, bitterness or toxicity, purification techniques or assay methods knowing inhibitory concentration. Also, the clinical significance in term of decrease in systolic blood pressure (SBP) is also provided in AHTPDB with major references.
